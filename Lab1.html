B. What is the probability that my background will give me a value that looks like my signal of 12.5 or greater?

C. $\displaystyle \textrm{Probability} = \int_{12.5}^{\infty} \textrm{pdf} (x)\, dx$ or $\displaystyle \textrm{probability} = 1 - \left(\int_{0}^{12.5} \textrm{pdf} (x)\, dx\right)$ since the pdf is normalized to 1, or 100%, and is well defined from 0 to 12.5 but not from 12.5 to $\infty$.

A. Poisson Distribution

The Poisson distribution is often used to describe the number of times an event occured in a given interval. The Poisson distribution takes in one parameter, $\mu\$, the shape parameter which is the average rate of events per unit interval.

D. The x-axis on the pdf graph would represent the potential values one could get in their experiment which by definition of the Poisson distribution, have to be descrete. In our example above we are looking at the probability of a rainy day and for our purposes it's either raining or its not raining, we don't care about a 0.25 rainy day, which makes our experiment descrete. This also means that our sigma has be be discrete since our accuracy can't be within half of a rainy day since we don't measure to a half of a rainy day.

E. Even though the results of the Poisson distribution are descrete you can still have a mean of 9.2, per the example in the lab question. This means that the mean falls inbetween 9 and 10, being more probable to get 9, since 9.2 is closer to 9 than 10.
